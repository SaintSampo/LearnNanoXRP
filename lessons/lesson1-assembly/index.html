What is the NanoXRP?

The NanoXRP is a small robot on wheels that you can program using a computer. It has motors, sensors, and a tiny computer brain — just like real robots used by engineers!

This lesson has 10 slides and a short quiz at the end. Take your time reading each step carefully.

🧰 What's in the Box?

Before you start building, let's make sure you have all the parts. Lay everything out on a flat surface and check each item off the list!

All your robot parts, laid out and ready to go

Parts Checklist

  • ✅ Chassis (the frame) – the body your robot sits on
  • ✅ 2 Hobby motors with encoders – these spin the wheels
  • ✅ 2 Wheels – attach to the motors
  • ✅ 1 Caster wheel – the little round wheel in the back
  • ✅ SparkFun XRP Controller Board – the robot's computer brain
  • ✅ Ultrasonic Distance Sensor – sees how far objects are
  • ✅ Line Follower Board – detects lines on the ground
  • ✅ Battery holder & batteries – the robot's power source
  • ✅ Screws and screwdriver – to hold it all together
  • ✅ USB cable – to connect to your computer
⚠️
Missing a part?

Raise your hand and ask your teacher before continuing. Don't try to build with missing pieces!

🦺 Safety First!

Building robots is awesome, but we need to be safe about it. Follow these rules every time you work with your robot.

1

No batteries during assembly

Keep the batteries OUT of the robot until you finish building and your teacher checks it. This prevents the motors from spinning at the wrong time.

2

Handle the circuit board carefully

The controller board has tiny electronic parts. Hold it by the edges and don't touch the metal parts on the bottom.

3

Don't force anything

Parts should fit together easily. If something doesn't fit, stop and ask for help — don't push or break it!

4

Keep screws in a cup or tray

Small screws love to roll off tables and disappear. Keep them safe in a small container while you work.

✅
Ready to build?

Once you've reviewed the safety rules, let's start putting the robot together!

Step 1: Attach the Motors

The motors are what make the wheels spin. Each motor clips into a slot on the sides of the chassis.

Slide each motor into its slot on the chassis until it clicks
1

Find the motor slots

Look on the sides of the chassis. You'll see two rectangular openings — one on the left and one on the right.

2

Slide each motor in

Push the motor into its slot with the motor shaft (the spinning part) pointing outward. The motor wires should point toward the inside of the chassis.

3

Secure with screws

Use the small screws to lock each motor in place. Tighten snugly but not too hard.

💡
Left and right motors

The motors are identical — either one can go on either side. Just make sure the wire connectors point toward the center of the robot.

Step 2: Attach the Wheels

Now it's time to give your robot some wheels!

Press each wheel firmly onto the motor shaft
1

Locate the motor shaft

The motor shaft is the little round rod sticking out from the motor. This is what the wheel will spin on.

2

Press the wheel on

Push the wheel onto the shaft. You'll need to press firmly — it should fit snugly. Wiggle it slightly side to side as you push.

3

Add the caster wheel

The caster wheel (the small round one) goes on the back of the chassis. Snap or screw it into its holder. This wheel helps the robot balance.

🤔
Why a caster wheel?

Real shopping carts have caster wheels too! They can swivel in any direction, which lets the robot turn smoothly without needing a third motor.

Step 3: Mount the Controller Board

The controller board is the brain of your robot. It's the green circuit board with the microcontroller chip on it.

The XRP Controller Board goes on top of the chassis
1

Find the mounting holes

The controller board has four small holes in the corners. The chassis also has four matching holes on top.

2

Line it up

Place the board on top of the chassis with the USB connector facing toward the back of the robot (toward the caster wheel).

3

Screw it down

Use four screws to attach the board to the chassis. Tighten them gently — the board can crack if you overtighten!

Step 4: Connect the Sensors

Your robot has two amazing sensors. Let's attach them!

The sonar sensor (front) and line follower (bottom) in place

Line Follower Board (Bottom)

This small board goes on the bottom front of the chassis. It uses infrared light to detect black lines on the floor.

  1. Slide the line follower board into its slot at the front-bottom of the chassis.
  2. Connect the small wire connector to the matching port on the controller board.

Ultrasonic Distance Sensor (Front)

This sensor looks like two robot eyes! It goes on the front of the robot and measures how far away things are.

  1. Snap the sensor into the front mount on the chassis. The two circular "eyes" should face forward.
  2. Connect the four-wire connector to the correct port on the controller board.
💡
Connector tip

Connectors only plug in one way — if it doesn't slide in easily, flip it around!

Step 5: Connect the Motors

Now connect the motor wires to the controller board. This lets the board tell the motors when to spin!

Plug the left motor wire into the LEFT port, and right motor into RIGHT
1

Find the motor ports

Look at the controller board for connectors labeled Motor 1 and Motor 2 (or Left and Right).

2

Connect left motor

Plug the left motor's wire connector into Motor 1 port. Hold the chassis facing you — the left motor is on your left.

3

Connect right motor

Plug the right motor's wire connector into Motor 2 port.

⚠️
Getting motors mixed up?

Don't worry! If the robot goes backward when you try to go forward, you can swap the motor connectors or fix it in software later.

Step 6: Add the Battery Pack

Almost done! The battery pack is what powers the robot. It attaches to the underside of the chassis.

The battery pack clips to the bottom of the chassis
1

Insert batteries

Open the battery holder and insert 4 AA batteries. Match the + and − symbols on the holder. Close the lid.

2

Attach to the chassis

Clip or screw the battery holder to the bottom of the chassis. The connector wire should reach the controller board.

3

Plug in — but keep power OFF

Connect the battery wire to the power port on the controller board. Make sure the power switch on the board is in the OFF position for now.

🎉
Almost there!

You've assembled your robot! Before turning it on, have your teacher check your work.
